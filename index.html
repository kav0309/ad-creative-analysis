Strategic Analysis

Key Findings

01
Format

Carousel vs Video — Two Different Funnels

Indē Wild runs mostly carousels, targeting high-intent scrollers who swipe through product details. Plum runs 100% video, optimising for Reels placement and cheaper CPM. Same category, completely different funnel stages being targeted.

02
Acquisition

Plum Has a New User Funnel. Indē Wild Doesn't.

Plum runs dedicated acquisition ads — a ₹599 trial kit and a free welcome kit — to lower the barrier for first-time buyers. Indē Wild's only visible offer is 20% off a full-price product. No low-risk entry point for new users.

03
Creative Age

Long-Running Ads Signal Top ROAS Performers

Indē Wild's two longest-running ads are both POV carousel variants — strong signal that the format + hook combination is their most efficient creative. Brands kill underperforming ads within 2–3 weeks; longevity = performance.

04
Distribution

Blinkit vs Flipkart — Different Distribution Bets

Indē Wild routes traffic to Blinkit, betting on quick-commerce impulse buying. Plum sends to both its own site and Flipkart, signalling marketplace dependence. Two completely different distribution philosophies visible directly in ad destinations.

05
Brand Identity

Locked Visual Identity vs Volume Strategy — The Clearest Gap

Indē Wild's entire ad library looks like one mood board: consistent beige/cream tones, clean product shots, minimal design. Plum switches between cinematic brand videos, raw UGC face-cams, and bold discount graphics in the same feed. Indē Wild is building a brand. Plum is optimising for reach. Both are valid — but they signal very different stages of brand maturity.
